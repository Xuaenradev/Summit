## 🌟 Highlights

- **Creative and planning in one place** — an infinite moodboard *and* Notion-style docs and databases, side by side. Stop paying for two tools.
- **Local-first, always** — your work lives on your machine as real files. No account, no cloud, no subscription. Works offline, forever.
- **Design Studio built in** — dithering, ASCII art, palette extraction, collage, and a full static design editor. Make the thing without leaving the app.
- **Collections** — Pinterest-style visual boards for gathering inspiration.
- **Mag** — build a printable magazine from your images and export it as a PDF.
- **Free.** No tiers, no card, no card-count limits.

## ℹ️ Overview

Summit is a local-first desktop project manager for people who plan *and* make things designers, game devs, students, engineers and who ever thinks it could work for them.

It exists because the tools I used didn't fit together. Notion is great for structure but has no creative surface. Milanote is a beautiful board but caps you at 100 cards and charges for more. Obsidian keeps your notes as real files but isn't a project manager. I wanted one app with the board, the docs, the databases, and the creative tools — that didn't rent me my own data back.

So I built it. Summit combines the block editor and databases of Notion, the infinite moodboard of Milanote, the markdown-on-disk philosophy of Obsidian, and a set of creative tools that don't exist in any of them. Everything runs on your machine. Your docs are plain markdown files you can open in any editor. Nothing is locked in.

This is a **beta**. It works, it's free, and it's the version I use every day.

## 🚀 What it does

**Boards** — an infinite canvas with notes, images, links, columns, to-dos, tables, embeds, drawing, and nested boards. Paste an image from your browser straight onto the board.

**Pages** — a block editor with headings, lists, toggles, callouts, code, embeds, wikilinks, and backlinks. Saved as markdown files on your disk.

**Databases** — the same data as a Table, Kanban board, Calendar, List, Gallery, or Timeline. Relations, rollups, and formulas included.

**Design Studio** — dithering (Floyd–Steinberg, Atkinson, Bayer), ASCII art, palette extraction, collage maker, and a layered design editor. Export it or drop it straight onto a board.

**Collections** — masonry grids for gathering visual inspiration.

**Mag** — arrange images into a multi-page magazine and export a print-ready PDF.

## ⬇️ Installation

**[⬇️ Download the latest release](https://github.com/Xuaenradev/summit/releases/latest)**

### Windows

1. Download `Summit_x64-setup.exe` from the [latest release](https://github.com/Xuaenradev/summit/releases/latest).
2. Run it.
3. **Windows will show a "Windows protected your PC" warning.** This is expected — Summit isn't code-signed yet (certificates cost money I haven't spent). Click **More info** → **Run anyway**.
4. Follow the installer. Summit appears in your Start menu.

**Requirements:** Windows 10 or 11 (64-bit).

### macOS and Linux

Not in this release. Summit is built with Tauri and runs on all three platforms — Mac and Linux builds are coming. ⭐ Star or watch this repo to know when they land.

## 🔒 Your data

Summit stores everything locally on your machine:

- **Pages** are plain `.md` markdown files you can open, edit, or back up with any tool.
- **Boards, databases, and settings** live in a local database in Summit's app folder.
- **Nothing is uploaded anywhere.** There's no account, no telemetry, no server. Summit works with your Wi-Fi off.

You own your work. If you ever stop using Summit, your notes are still just files.
